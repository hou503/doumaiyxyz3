Fitness can not only practice their favorite parts, mainly to the whole body coordination, can not fall weak parts

Posted on 2021-04-09

Everyone has more or less weak muscle groups, some people’s chest muscles are weak, some people’s shoulders are not wide, some people’s arms are not good, and so on. If we don’t carry out targeted training for weak muscle groups, it will affect our physique. Today, I’d like to give you a detailed introduction to “weak points” and how to improve them.

Weak points
“Weak point” refers to the strength defect caused by the backward development of a certain muscle group in compound movements; From the physical point of view, it shows a weak muscle group. And * * isolated action and auxiliary action * * can improve this situation.

For the purpose of muscle hypertrophy
From the perspective of muscle hypertrophy, the weak point refers to the “underdevelopment” phenomenon of a certain muscle group in the body.
In many cases, weak spots are caused by genes. For example, you may be born with a narrow chest and a wide pelvis, which requires more effort in the development of shoulder and leg muscles to reduce the “narrow” feeling of the body and achieve the “X” shape. Most of the time, you make up for your inborn weakness by trying to make your muscles bigger.
However, the weak points are also acquired. For example, sometimes your body structure may not be suitable for doing certain movements, and if you insist on taking these movements as the focus of training, it is not only easy to get injured, but also not the best choice for muscle hypertrophy.
Physical weakness is often caused by the way the action is performed. When doing push down movements, some muscles of the trainer’s back may not get any stimulation, or the biceps brachii may become the fastest growing part. In a similar situation, the trainer may need to adjust the grip distance, angle, and movement variation to find a more suitable method of action execution.
Good movement quality is very important for training. According to the research findings, as a complex action that requires the cooperation of multiple muscle groups and joints, a muscle group is not in place due to muscle activation, which may lead to unbalanced muscle development.

For the purpose of absolute power
Strength lifters will also have a “weak link in the chain” in a certain movement. For example, the trainer may be able to pull more weight with the help belt. So this shows that the grip strength is the factor limiting the hard pull strength of the trainer. But increasing the hard pull (training more frequently, or increasing the hard pull capacity) is not the best way to improve grip strength. On the contrary, the best way to improve grip strength is to add grip strength training in daily hard pull training.
This situation is one of the countless problems that strength lifters may encounter. The way to solve this problem is to add non three major movements in training. If only training three movements, it is easy to cause the imbalance of muscle development, resulting in the lack of training capacity. Adding non three auxiliary movements to make up for the lack of training capacity can exercise muscles in a balanced way, so that there is no “weak point” in the movement of strength lift.
However, when the trainer is faced with some special circumstances (such as injuries, etc.), the training plan needs to be adjusted accordingly. According to the individual differences and the training period, the proportion of auxiliary movements in the total capacity can fluctuate between 25% and 50%.
