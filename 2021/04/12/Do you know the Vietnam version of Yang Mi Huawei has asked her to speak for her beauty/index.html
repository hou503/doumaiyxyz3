Action one up tilt dumbbell push

Action tips: * adjust the angle of dumbbell stool to 45 degrees with the ground. Then lie down on the stool with your shoulder blades tightened. Hold the dumbbell with both hands, palms of both hands opposite, keeping the core tight. The up tilt dumbbell push is a little different from the usual practice, that is, the hand is close to the body, so we usually do the up tilt dumbbell push, the elbow is outward, about 45 degrees with the body. When inhaling, bend your elbow and put it down, push it up again when it reaches the lowest point, and exhale at the same time, 12-15 times in each group, four groups.
**Action 2 dumbbell flying bird

Action tips: * * this action is called up oblique dumbbell flying bird, so its angle is the same as the above action. The ramp is at a 45 degree angle to the ground, and then lies on the bench, keeping the core tight. The whole shoulder blade sinks, and then the dumbbell is grasped with both hands. Our hands are opened from both sides of the body. Then put the elbow down, keep the arm slightly bent, don’t stretch too straight. When we reach the lowest point, feel our chest muscles, and there will be a sense of stretching. Inhale at the same time and stop at the lowest point. Then exhale and lift up, 12-15 times in each group, four groups.
**Action 3: dumbbell push on yoga mat

**Action tips: * first prepare a yoga mat, then lie on the yoga mat, bend your knees, tighten your shoulder blades, and tighten your core. After holding the dumbbell with both hands, slightly bend the elbow down, inhale at the same time, and then push the dumbbell up. When pushing up, keep the elbow slightly bent, not completely straight. Pay attention to feel the power of chest muscle in the whole process, 12 times in each group, four groups.
