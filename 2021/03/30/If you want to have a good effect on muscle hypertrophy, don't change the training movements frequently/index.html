If you want to have a good effect on muscle hypertrophy, don't change the training movements frequently

Posted on 2021-03-30

In the muscle hypertrophy training, some trainers like to change the training action frequently, while others will knock on one action. But in fact, these two training methods may not be able to achieve the ideal muscle effect. So the question is, how should we choose the right training method? Let’s take a look at Dr. Eric Helms, author of the book of muscles and strength, researcher of New Zealand Institute of performance and nsca-cscs, on this issue.

Unfamiliar movements are less effective for muscle hypertrophy
In the world of bodybuilding training, many people would suggest changing movements after a period of training, so as to keep the muscles adapting and growing. Many bodybuilders call it “muscle fusion” and take it as one of the core contents of training. But in reality, from some angles, the situation is just the opposite. When you are not proficient in a certain movement, the effect of muscle augmentation will become worse.
When training new movements (especially complex movements), the initial strength growth mainly (but not all) comes from * * neuromuscular adaptability * . Only when the movement technique is mastered, can the trainer expose the muscle to gradual overload to achieve the effect of muscle hypertrophy effectively. Therefore, even if your training goal is to increase muscle, you also need to have a relatively high degree of proficiency in your training movements to achieve a good training effect.
On the other hand, if you keep changing training movements, you may never be able to master any of them. On the contrary, it will make training inefficient.

*A certain degree of diversity is important**
Although the effect of unfamiliar movements on muscle hypertrophy is poor, it is not to say that changing training movements has no value at all. In fact, the diversity of training movements is very important to improve the efficiency of muscle hypertrophy, but ensuring the diversity of movements is different from confusing muscles, and the premise is to master the training movements skillfully.
In the training movement, each compound movement needs a lot of different joint and muscle cooperation. However, doing this compound action directly may not be the best choice for training every muscle group used. This is like pulling a chain, where it breaks must be the weakest link.
Therefore, there is a weak muscle group when doing a compound action, which may become a factor restricting strength. Therefore, by doing some auxiliary actions, to ensure a more balanced and comprehensive development of muscles, and not because a muscle is weak, and affect the strength. This is very important in terms of absolute strength and muscle hypertrophy.

Automatic adjustment action selection
In any training, the trainer needs to clearly understand “what action is more suitable for his body”, and automatically adjust the action selection, so that the training effect will be twice the result with half the effort.
If your training goal is muscle hypertrophy, the main compound movements should allow you to judge your progress speed (at least an upper limb push, an upper limb pull, a squat, and a hip hinge movement), and at least maintain several medium cycles or even a large cycle in the training; The isolated action can be switched between training day and training day, or between medium cycle and medium cycle.
For more details about muscle and strength, please pay attention to the book muscle and strength: building a complete body-building knowledge system with rigorous science.
