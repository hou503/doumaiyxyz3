It's not just relaxing. It helps you adjust to your best

Posted on 2021-03-23

Pre competition weight reduction is a common strategy, which is often used not only by strength lifters and Olympic weightlifters, but also in other events, such as triathlon, track and field, endurance competition, etc. as long as it is carried out in a single day, this strategy can be used.

Pre competition weight reduction means using the method of reducing training capacity to achieve the effect of reducing fatigue and improving potential (fitness), which will be directly reflected in sports performance. The pre game reduction is very similar to the reduction mentioned before, but they have different purposes.
The goal of reduction is usually to continue to make progress by reducing fatigue.
The purpose of pre competition weight reduction is to make athletes reach the best sports state on competition day. This process is also called “sprint”
**How to reduce the weight before the competition
When there is about one to four weeks to go before the game, reduce the weight before the game. The specific length of the pre competition reduction period should be determined according to the length of the overload cycle and your accumulated fatigue level.
During the pre competition period, reduce the training capacity by at least 13 and at most 23.
During the pre competition tapering period, the training intensity remains unchanged, or only slightly decreases (no more than 10% decrease). Or, if you use a longer pre race reduction period (3-4 weeks), the training intensity can be increased slightly as part of the linear cycle plan of “reduced capacity, increased intensity”.

When the competition is about to begin, the reduction of training capacity can also be achieved through a direct rest one to three days before the competition.
However, some studies have shown that a relatively easy “preparation training” in 24-48 hours before the competition may further help sports performance. Therefore, it is better to have a training with very low capacity and RPE one or two days before the competition.
The above way is one of the most effective ways to reduce the amount before the game based on the current scientific research, which can let you play to the maximum potential on the day of the game.

Here is an example of a simple pre competition weight reduction scheme for a strength lifter. Suppose that the athlete starts to reduce weight two weeks before the competition.
Reference example of pre competition reduction
When there are still two weeks to go before the competition, start to arrange the three major events in some training days, and try to refer to the competition order (squat, bench push and hard pull). Use low frequency (1-3 times) training, but keep the intensity at the weight you usually use when doing 4-6 times (75% - 85% of 1RM). By doing so, you can maintain the intensity of your training without losing strength, and at the same time speed up your recovery through low RPE. In addition, by concentrating the three events on the same day, the training frequency of each main event is directly increased, which not only increases the specificity of the competition, but also prevents the training capacity from decreasing too fast due to the decrease of the number of times.
Reduce the number of times in each group to reduce the main movement capacity by 50% ~ 70%.
Continue with the auxiliary action, but halve the number of groups for all actions.
RM: the ultimate power of times

In the small cycle with 2 weeks to go, try to lift the handle weight. What it means here is to simulate the start of the game on the Saturday before the game. At this time, the RPE will generally reach 7.5 ~ 8.5.
The same content of training should be carried out in the week of the competition and the week before the competition, but the last training should be carried out 1-2 days before the competition. The main action only needs to be carried out once to reach 4-5 RPE. Do not do any other actions including auxiliary actions. In addition, the last “training day” of this week does not exist, because this training day is your competition day.
RPE: subjective feeling
Finally, don’t forget the importance of weight selection in the competition. If you are not rational, no matter how well you do in training and pre match reduction, you may “step on thunder” on match day.
